Understanding Your Bill

Your OWCD electric bill has two components: a fixed monthly service charge and a variable energy charge based on your actual consumption.

Non-residential customers also pay a demand charge based on peak 15-minute power draw during the billing period.

  • The minimum bill applies even at zero usage â€” it covers grid infrastructure costs
  • Underground service has a slightly higher energy rate due to installation costs
  • Commercial customers pay a lower energy rate but a demand charge for peak loads
  • Arizona summers typically drive Julyâ€“September bills 30â€“50% higher than winter
  • Solar customers can significantly offset energy charges â€” see our DG program

About OWCD

The Ocotillo Water Conservation District (OWCD) was established by order of the Maricopa County, Arizona Board of Supervisors on March 19, 1968. The District is organized under Title 48, Chapter 19 of the Arizona Revised Statutes and operates as a municipal corporation with general statutory powers.

Located in southern Maricopa County, the District historically served irrigation pumping loads of agricultural customers farming approximately 5,180 acres. As the Ocotillo area has urbanized â€” now encompassing the Robson Community, Bashas' Corporate Office, Snedigar Sportsplex, and Ocotillo Golf Resort â€” the customer mix has shifted toward residential and commercial loads.

Power is sourced from a combination of hydroelectric resources (including generation tied to the Colorado River system) and solar energy, reflecting the District's commitment to clean, reliable power in the Arizona desert.

OWCD is governed by a three-member Board of Directors, elected at large within the District. Current District staff consists of one part-time District Manager and three part-time employees.
